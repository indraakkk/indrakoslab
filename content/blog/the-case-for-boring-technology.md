Placeholder post — swap in your real writing. Every stack decision is a loan: you get leverage today and pay interest every time something breaks at 2am. New frameworks charge the highest rates because nobody has documented their failure modes yet.

> Innovation tokens are scarce. Spend them on your product, not your plumbing.

My rule of thumb: pick the boring option unless the exciting one solves a problem you can name today — not one you might have at some imagined scale. Postgres over the new database. A queue you know over the one trending this month.

The payoff is not glamour, it is velocity that survives contact with production. Boring tech means answers on Stack Overflow, upgrade paths that work, and juniors who can contribute in week one.
